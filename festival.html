Most Popular Festivals of India

Diwali-

Festival of Lights

This pan Indian festival, famous for lighting up our lives, Diwali is joyously celebrated globally now.Sending a powerful message of the triumph of good over evil, the festival is celebrated in every nook and corner of the country.

EXPLORE MORE

Holi-

Festival of Colors

The Pandora box of wholesome fun and good natured pranks characterise the atmosphere in the colourful festival of Holi. This festival is also slowly gaining popularity in the global sphere. Holi is celebrated by throwing and applying colours on each other, in myriad hues of jubilance.

EXPLORE MORE

Christmas-

Birth of Jesus Christ

The birth of Jesus Christ is celebrated with ecstatic merriment and wonderful joy. The warm smell of walnut cakes can be inhaled in every household. As if captivated by a confetti of Santa caps, Christmas sees the prevalence of these caps and reindeer headgear decorating the heads of civilians.

EXPLORE MORE

Dussehra-

Vijayadasami

The culmination of the 9 days of Navrati, end in the 10th day of Dussehra. The key attraction of this festival is the burning down of the effigies of Ravan and his two brothers, symbolic of when Lord Rama destroys Ravan in the epic tale of Ramayana.

EXPLORE MORE

Durga Puja-

Durgotsava or Navaratri

A festival celebrating the powerful Goddess Durga. The colourful and high energy festival is celebrated by decorating huge pandals, in the middle of which a heavily decorated idol of Goddess Durga sits.

EXPLORE MORE

Onam-

Harvest Festival of Kerala

An important festival of Kerala, the festival of Onam is a very colourful affair.People celebrate by decorating their house with various floral arrangements and woman wear the gorgeous white and golden border sarees.
